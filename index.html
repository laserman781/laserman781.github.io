    Leadership & Community Engagements

    YMCA Nicklebelt Budokan Judo Club

    Judo, translated as "gentle way", teaches the principle of flexibility in the application of the technique, skill and timing, rather than the use of force brutal. JUDO participates in the physical and intellectual development of children, gives the confidence to make them independent. The child is socialized and realizes his full potential. He develops concentration, motor skills and balance. He learns to channel his energy and become aware of his strengths and weaknesses in order to defend himself, to be confident and to fall without getting hurt. In judo classes, you can learn how to lead, rather than using pure strength to overcome a stronger opponent.

    I started volunteering at the age of 10, helping to teach Judo at the Sudbury YMCA when the program was at risk of closing because the head instructor (black belt) had left. Rather than going to another club, I made the conscious decision to stay and help an intermediate (brown belt) instructor keep the program going and growing by helping to teach beginners until a new head instructor joined. Five years later, the Judo program has become the Sudbury YMCA's largest ever volunteer run program, offering 6 classes a week and teaching more than 120 children, all thanks to an annual collective investment of over 4000 volunteer hours to which I contribute.

    Opera singer with Opera for a Cause, Opera Gala
